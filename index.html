Aims And Objective

I am African, I am young Cameroonian. My passion, my choice of orientation and the availability of even a smartphone are more than enough to make me a scholar, a professional, a young trained and qualified So I think I am, I reason then I am free, I am but I seem to be a dunce.
I believe in learning that goes far beyond educational clichés in society, I believe that I can forget an iron throne in the sun without going through the mold of  conventional school, I believe in the possibility of being because I reason.
Long before Jules Ferry sent us for a walk, I thought, I reasoned, and I always summer.
Why limit myself to methods, to canvas that suit me like in a boubou? Why do I have to be called a societal deregulation because I refuse to be "trained". If it is true that I am deformed, then I prefer myself to my entourage settled and returned zombies and automata.
I have a curiosity that has never been thwarted by the school, Jules Ferry and his dark ambitions; I am endowed with unparalleled flexibility and self-discipline. Come on  complain, I brag about it!
I am passionate about discovery, experience, and the unknown. I create a future for  me, my brothers, my sisters, my cousins, my cousins, my uncles, my aunts, my friends  my comrades, I create opportunities for my society, for my community. I am  African and I assume the prejudices and the taunts.
I said it, I campaign for an African education, I fight for an African education ..
